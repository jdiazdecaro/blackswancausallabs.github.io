Why the Black Swan

Rare events reshape everything

In probability theory, a black swan is an event that is rare, carries extreme impact, and is only rationalized in hindsight. For centuries, it was assumed all swans were white until a single observation in Australia shattered that certainty. In medicine, the most consequential insights often follow the same pattern: hidden in messy, real-world data, invisible to conventional methods, and transformative once revealed.

Real-World Data

The raw material

Data collected outside of traditional clinical trials, drawn from the routine delivery of healthcare and patient experience.

  • Electronic health records (EHR)
  • Insurance claims & billing data
  • Patient registries & biobanks
  • Wearables & digital health devices
  • Patient-reported outcomes

Real-World Evidence

The insight derived

Clinical evidence about the use, benefits, and risks of medical products, generated from the analysis of real-world data through rigorous methodology.

  • Comparative effectiveness studies
  • Post-market safety surveillance
  • Treatment pattern analyses
  • Health technology assessments
  • Regulatory decision support
