Every Wednesday morning, an autonomous AI agent searches PubMed and bioRxiv for the most noteworthy recent papers at the intersection of real-world evidence, causal inference, and artificial intelligence. It selects five, writes a short opinionated take on each, updates this page, and sends a newsletter to subscribers. No human curator picks the papers from week to week unless there is a seminal or highly relevant paper that should be included.

This is intentional. Black Swan Causal Labs exists to help scientists use AI in evidence work responsibly, and that means being transparent about how we use it ourselves.

  • SearchThe agent runs structured queries across PubMed and bioRxiv covering RWE, target trial emulation, causal inference, machine learning in pharmacoepidemiology, and large language models in RWE research.
  • CurateIt scans the candidate set, identifies the papers most relevant to practitioners, and narrows the field to five that span methods, applications, and tooling.
  • WriteFor each paper the agent drafts a two or three sentence take, focused on what is interesting or new and what readers should pay attention to. Not summaries. Takes.
  • Publish and sendIt commits the new entries to the website repository and delivers the newsletter to subscribers through Buttondown.

Editorial oversight comes from John Diaz-Decaro, who designed the search strategy, defined the curation criteria, and reviews outputs over time to refine the system. The agent is the curator. The human is the editor and the accountable scientist.
